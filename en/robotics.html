Development of Autonomous Mobile robot platform

Private company - Busan, South Korea

2022~2023

limited information due to a non-disclosure agreement


Creation of the robotic department and the 1st autonomous ground vehicle of the company.
Developed with 4 international interns a ROS then ROS2 based UGV with semi-auto mapping, autonomous navigation and basic person follower.
The robot implements 2 wheels differential drive with LiDAR based SLAM. Modeling are done in Solidworks and simulations in Gazebo.

Keywords:
LiDAR, BLDC motor, RPi4, Arduino nano, ROS, ROS2, Gazebo, AMCL, cartographer, IMU, sensor fusion, Flask, Vue.js

Mechanical points

We used 2 hub motors with integrated hard wheels and an encoder with 2 caster wheels arranged in a diamond shape.
The frame was designed in Solidworks and 2 frames were made: 1 in stainless steel by a workshop and I made a similar frame using 20mm aluminum profile tubes.
I made all the wiring, soldering, connectors and frame cuts, and several tests were run on site in S.Korea.

Algorithm points

This solution used common ROS2 algorithms, such as cartographer for mapping and amcl for navigation.
A simple RGB based algorithm was developed using C++ and OpenCV for target tracking.

Web server points

I developed a basic python based web server and Vue.js front end to run the different control algorithms, to change some algorithm parameters and to initialize the target following mode as well as the live feed from the embedded camera.

3D prototype creation for robotic partial mastectomy surgery

TegnyTech - for Yonsei Cancer Hospital

2019~2020 & 2021

limited information due to patent in progress (owned by Yonsei)


Design of a prototype of surgical tool for robotic mastectomy with Yonsei Surgeon and Professor and creation of a 3D model in order to apply for a device patent

Keywords:
Mechatronics, Robotic surgical tool, Mastectomy, Prototyping, 3D Model, Professor design meeting

1st prototype

The first prototype was a design of an end tool for specimen retrieval during breast surgery.
This design was based on surgical cables and pulley systems.
I had the chance to have several online meetings with the Professor and Doctor as well as joined him for an on-site surgery.

2nd prototype

The second prototype was a dynamic endoscope and tools holder for manual surgery.
The design included several 1 or 3 degrees of rotation articulations as well as motors and pulley systems
Most of the meetings were held online, and the final meeting for validation happened in Seoul.
