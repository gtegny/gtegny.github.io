Monitoring and control system for negative pressure rooms

TegnyTech - For Uiwang health center, South Korea

2020~2021

limited information due to a non-disclosure agreement


Due to rising COVID-19, Uiwang decided to create a new testing center with 2 high negative pressure rooms for sampling and a negative hall for medical staffs.
This system includes 2 main AHUs with automation to build (PLC based), up to 12 CAVs and VAVs to monitor and control to achieve the required pressure difference requirements. Communication between the different devices uses MODBUS RTU/TCP and BACnet IP.
I was in charge, with my partner company, of the design and commissioning of the negative pressure system as well as the heating / cooling system. I worked on all the communication and supervision parts as well as the logics, and my partner developed the local controllers.
We checked the hardwares installation and did the commissioning together on site.



Keywords:
BACnet IP, MODBUS RTU/TCP, BEMS, PLC, GUI, JavaScript, Webserver, AHU, CHU, VAV

Automation points

The automation panels for AHU contain a PLC for the temperature sensor readings, the control and reading of valves and dampers using PID algorithms. The PLC also receives statuses of the CHU and the room controllers to adjust its setpoints, and converts these values from MODBUS RTU to a MODBUS TCP or a BACNET IP connection with the main BEMS.
CHUs had their own custom made controller made by my partner and allows local control of air flow and air temperature. The CHUs also received the temperature and fans control modes via proprietary protocol from the room controllers.

Building management system

I used the Schneider Electric BEMS system to read the different equipments statuses via MODBUS TCP and BACnet IP. I also created graphic UIs, that are accessible via a webpage, to monitor and control all setpoints in the system:
Main chiller mode and setpoints, OHUs control mode / fan speed / temperature setpoints / alarms, CHUs fan speed / temperature setpoints, main control mode (auto or manual).

Heating / cooling ventilation system for Hospital 9 floor

TegnyTech - For Uiwang hospital, South Korea

2019~2020


The goal of the project was the designed and implementation of a new 9-floorÂ hospital building ventilation system.
The ventilation system contains 19 OHUs, 18 EHUs, up to 190 CHUs that we created. We also integrate 20 FCUs and 1 heating pump for water cooling / heating, from external companies, into the monitoring and control system.
The system allows the easy monitoring and control of every device via a technical graphic user interface, but it also contains more user friendly floorplan type graphics that show real time statuses of each floors and rooms in the hospital.
The system runs autonomously with the different room control setpoints, air quality measurements and Season mode. Control of setpoints by scheduling is also available as well as manual overwriting and alarming system.

I worked on this project with my company in collaboration with my partner company and his customer.
I designed and built the 19 OHUs automation panels, all the graphic interfaces and control programs as well as set all the communications via the different devices.
I worked for 6 months at his office in Seoul, then we checked the devices installation, did the communication wiring and the commissioning together on site.



Keywords:
PLC, AHU, CHU, Valve, PID, GUI, BEMS, Automation, Control panel, BACnet IP, MODBUS RTU/TCP

Automation points

I designed and built 19 automation panels for the control of the OHUs. It contains power converters, connection terminals, relays and a PLC. The PLC received temperature sensors values, Air quality information via MODBUS RTU, CHUs and FCUs statuses via MODBUS RTU, differential pressure values for filter status. It controls via PIDs the air damper position, cooled/heated water valve, CHUs & FCUs temperature and fan speed, control mode of the heating pump.
The room controllers control the temperature setpoints and fans speed of the local CHUs device, created by my partner company. An air quality multi-sensor device, created by my partner company, was installed on a specific floor.
All the devices were connected to the main BEMS installed in the basement on 1 computer. The CHUs, the air quality monitoring device and EHUs were connected to the OHU PLC via MODBUS RTU, the OHU PLCs were connected to the BEMS via BACnet IP, the external FCUs and the heating pump were connected to the BEMS via MODBUS TCP.

Schneider Electric Healthcare Building segment: Global solution demonstration used worldwide

Schneider Electric - Boston, United States

2013~2014


The Ecostruxure Healthcare demo is a marketing platform and application that shows the integration of the different Schneider Electric products for every level of decision for the healthcare sector: Hospital building management and Pharmaceutical factories. Instead of video and static documents, this set of interconnected virtual machines runs the real Schneider Electric products, and simulates the control of a realistic hospital and shows the response of the system to real-life scenario.

I worked on this project with the Global Solution Architect, responsible for the segment worldwide. The Global solution architect defined the project and scenario, and I coded all the GUI with svg graphics and JavaScript interactive components, algorithms via block logics and scripts, and the communication to integrate the others software.
We checked the hardwares installation and did the commissioning together on site.
I saw this demonstration be used even 5 years after its creation at an automation fair at the COEX in Seoul.

BEMS, Automation, digital twins, Schneider Electric, lighting control, Ventilation control, access control, Demonstration, Integration, Healthcare building, Prototyping
